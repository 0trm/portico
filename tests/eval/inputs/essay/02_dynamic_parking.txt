Cities should price street parking dynamically. The current flat-rate system creates a tragedy of the commons: at peak hours, every space is full and drivers circle the block hunting for one, generating up to 30% of urban traffic. Dynamic pricing -- raise the rate until ~15% of spaces are open -- eliminates the hunt, cuts congestion, and prices the curb at its true value. San Francisco's SFpark pilot showed exactly this effect, with double-digit reductions in cruising and no net change in revenue per occupied space.
