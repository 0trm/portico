Grid-Scale Battery Project Approved in Northern Chile

The provincial energy authority approved a 750-megawatt-hour grid battery project near Antofagasta on Tuesday, the largest such installation announced in South America this year. Construction is expected to begin in March and finish by late 2027.

The project, led by a joint venture of Engie and a Chilean state utility, will store solar generation from the Atacama Desert and discharge it during evening demand peaks. Officials said the battery will reduce reliance on natural-gas peaker plants in the region by an estimated 18 percent.

"Storage is the missing piece," said Patricia Gallardo, the regional energy director, in a press briefing. "Solar without storage forces us to keep fossil capacity online for evenings. This project closes the gap."

The approval comes amid a broader push by the government to retire coal plants ahead of a 2030 deadline. The Antofagasta project will receive concessional financing from a multilateral development bank and is expected to create 1,200 construction jobs at peak.

Environmental groups praised the decision but called for clearer rules on lithium sourcing, given that nearby salt flats are themselves contested mining sites.
