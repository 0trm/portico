We investigate whether transformer attention sparsity correlates with retrieval accuracy on long-context tasks. Using a stratified sample of open-weights checkpoints across three model families (Llama, Mistral, Qwen), we train layer-wise linear probes to predict the gold passage from attention activations. We find that retrieval-relevant heads concentrate in the bottom third of the network and that their sparsity (Gini coefficient on attention weights) is significantly higher (p<0.01) in checkpoints that score above 80% on needle-in-a-haystack benchmarks. The effect persists across families and is robust to held-out evaluation. We release the probe set, raw activations, and replication scripts.
